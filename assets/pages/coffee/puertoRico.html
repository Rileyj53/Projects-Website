Puerto Rico


Quick Facts

  • Coffee was brought to Puerto Rico from Martinique in 1736 1
  • Produces beans noted for their balanced body and acidity, as well as a fruity aroma. 1
  • Coffee was introduced during Spanish colonial rule 3
  • Coffee is considered one of the basic needs in the culture 3
  • Some farmers focus on high-quality, specialty coffee 3

Coffee was first introduced to the region under Spanish colonial rule. When it was introduced it wasn’t considered a very important crop. In the 19th century population and hurricanes had a negative impact on coffee production. By 1950 coffee production couldn’t keep up with the demand on the island let alone exports. Today Puerto Rican coffee farmers focus more on high quality small batch specialty coffee as opposed to mass production for exports.3 This gives Puerto Rican coffee a very unique place in the world of coffee. Puerto Rican coffee traditionally has a balanced body and acidity, with a fruity aroma. This makes Puerto Rican coffee a rare sight to see, especially because they only produce about 17% of the coffee produced worldwide.4
