Costa Rica


Quick Facts

  • Only produce Arabic coffee beans1
  • medium body and sharp acidity often described as the perfect balance 1

Coffee was first introduced to Costa Rica in the late 1700s when plants were gifted to anyone willing to grow them. The country has very mineral-rich volcanic soils and cool mountain climates these are the ideal conditions for growing coffee. Once Costa Rica had gained independence from Spain coffee exports helped fun lots of infrastructure in the country. But coffee also played a huge role in the culture as well everyone drank coffee from the farmer to the politician. The country only produces Arabica beans which are generally smoother and more balanced.7
