Beginning in the late 1700s Colombia quickly became a major exporter of coffee on the global scale. In 1977 the National Federation of Coffee Growers of Colombia was created to protect the interests of coffee growers, implementing a logistics system that enabled rural, small-estate farmers to export their coffee more efficiently. Since Colombia’s climate and elevation varies a lot the flavor of the coffee also varies with it. Lower and higher altitude coffee has a full body and deeper floral notes. While middle elevation tends to be well-rounded with nutty and chocolate flavors, a gentle sweetness, and a mellow acidity. Locals enjoy coffee much more than the rest of the world, they enjoy it at night not emphasizing the caffeine. This means coffee shops are open later and some aren’t even open in the mornings. 8
