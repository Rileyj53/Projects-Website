Coffee was first brought to the country by Dutch traders in the late 1600s. They smuggled the seeds from Yemen and brought them to Indonesia. In the mid-1800s a disease knows as Coffee rust affected the crops destroying plantations on the region. To save the industry the Dutch planted different disease-resistant coffee species, robusta. Unfortunately, the farms were plagued by poverty, starvation, and inhumane working conditions. By 1942 the Dutch were forced from Indonesia and that significantly improved working conditions. When the Dutch left the government gave the plantations to the laborers giving them an opportunity to grow the industry. 11
