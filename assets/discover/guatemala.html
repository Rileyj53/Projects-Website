Guatemala


Quick Facts

  • Distinctive taste quality, very rich flavor1
  • three main growing regions, Antigua, Coban and Huehuetanango1
  • Coffee from the region is full bodied, taste has notes of spice and chocolate1
  • Coffee has been one of their most important crops since the early 1800s5
  • 10% of coffee produced in Guatemala stays in Guatemala5

Coffee was first introduced to the region in the 1800s to alleviate the decline in indigo and cochineal exports. By the late 20th century coffee accounted for 90% of the regions exports. Now Guatemala ranks 2nd in the world in terms of coffee production.6 Locals in Guatemala like to enjoy coffee from their own region as posed to importing it from other countries. Artisanal coffee has skyrocketed in the past decade with new coffee shops popping up all the time, all with the same focus, highlighting the great coffee Guatemala has to offer. 5Guatemalan coffee is traditionally more acidic than other regions, this id due to the volcanic soil and the elevation. This makes Guatemalan coffee perfect for espresso and dark roasts, as opposed to lighter roasts
